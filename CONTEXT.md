# JHD Astro Website Context

This context defines the public-site language for Jason Holt Digital as the
official hub for the Mythic GME apps. It exists so product copy, About-page
copy, resources, support, and future planning use the same terms and do not
drift back into portfolio, resume, or generic app-marketing language.

## Language

### Site role

**Official Mythic GME Apps Hub**:
The public website for the licensed Mythic GME mobile and desktop apps, their
support material, community paths, and maker story.
_Avoid_: Portfolio site, personal brand site, generic studio homepage.

**Product-First Site**:
A site whose main job is to help players understand, trust, get, and use the
Mythic apps.
_Avoid_: Employer-facing site, resume site, general founder narrative.

**Player**:
A person using Mythic in the middle of play, story, decisions, and imagination.
_Avoid_: Customer, consumer, generic user when play context matters.

**Mythic Community**:
The existing community of Mythic players whose feedback, requests, bug reports,
reviews, and beta testing shape the apps.
_Avoid_: Audience, market, customer base.

**Player Trust**:
The confidence a player develops when the site shows the apps are official,
maintained, useful in play, and shaped by real community feedback.
_Avoid_: Brand credibility, marketing authority, social proof alone.

### Voice and wording

**Site Voice**:
Direct, concrete, conversational copy that sounds like Jason after just enough
editing for grammar, clarity, and length.
_Avoid_: Corporate gloss, essay openings, over-polish, defensive caveats.

**Plainspoken Imagination**:
The copy mode for personal story moments: honest about imagination, games,
worlds, and story without sounding poetic or inflated.
_Avoid_: Word soup, lyrical abstraction, phrases Jason would not say out loud.

**No Claim-Colon Explanation Pattern**:
Do not write prose that makes a claim and then uses a colon to restate, list, or
explain the claim. Use ordinary sentences with the explanation integrated before
or after the claim.
_Avoid_: "Mythic was a tool for the kind of play I loved: making a character...",
"The lesson was this: ...", "The pattern is: claim, colon, explanation."

**Say The Actual Thing**:
Public copy should make a real point in plain language. If a sentence does not
add a specific claim, evidence, constraint, or decision, cut it or ask what the
real point is.
_Avoid_: Impressive-sounding filler, vague value language, abstract polish,
padded explanations, saying a lot without saying anything.

**Cliche Allergy**:
The copy constraint that popular professional labels must be earned by concrete
work before they appear.
_Avoid_: Personal brand, thought leader, builder as vibe, product-minded as
filler, overusing shipped or shipping as generic proof.

**Story Hook**:
An opening move that earns attention through Jason's real path into product
work before naming role labels.
_Avoid_: Title-first pitch, credential-first opening, generic founder summary.

**Concrete Before Abstract**:
The copy rule that player-facing evidence should appear before broad claims
about craft, product judgment, or role identity.
_Avoid_: Slogan-first copy, abstract positioning without proof, polished claims
that could belong to anyone.

**Player Outcome Line**:
A sentence that says what changes for the player in play.
_Avoid_: Feature inventory, implementation detail, generic usefulness claim.

**Role Label Placement**:
The rule that professional labels appear only after concrete product and trust
evidence has given them meaning.
_Avoid_: Role label in the H1, role label in the page subtitle, title-first
positioning.

### About page

**Maker Trust Story**:
The About page narrative that shows who builds the official apps, why players
can trust him, how the community shapes the work, and where the product is
going.
_Avoid_: Full biography, portfolio pitch, employer-facing case study.

**Maker's Note**:
A short first-person introduction that humanizes the product without pulling
the page away from player outcomes.
_Avoid_: Founder manifesto, resume summary, technical process essay.

**Player-Listening Builder**:
The About-page framing that Jason's authority comes from building and
maintaining the licensed apps, listening closely to Mythic players, and
protecting their play experience.
_Avoid_: Caveat-first bio, implying veteran-player authority, self-disqualifying
copy.

**Imaginative Play Origin**:
The personal story direction that connects Jason's childhood games of
imagination with his brother, characters from imagination and Legos, stories
like Redwall, The Lord of the Rings, The Black Cauldron, and Star Wars, video
game RPGs, and early code as ways to explore unknown worlds and interesting
characters.
_Avoid_: Grand origin myth, resume arc, teacher-first framing, implying tabletop
veteran authority, making the story sound more polished than Jason would say.

**About Opening Sentence**:
The approved direction for the first personal sentence on the About page:
Jason and his brother grew up playing games of imagination, getting lost in
stories like Redwall and The Lord of the Rings, creating characters from
imagination and Legos, and exploring unknown worlds and interesting characters
in video game RPGs.
_Avoid_: Career-first opening, teacher-first opening, abstract imagination
language, polished phrasing Jason would not say.

**About Header Promise**:
The approved About-page header direction: use a page-level title such as
"About Jason," then say Jason builds the official Mythic GME apps and keeps
them close to the people who play them.
_Avoid_: Fuzzy co-creation claims, repeating full license proof, "shaped by the
community" when the more precise point is staying close to players and real
sessions, or using "Hi, I'm Jason" as the page title when the Profile section
also introduces Jason.

**About Section Heading Discipline**:
The page header should name the page ("About Jason"), and the first body
section should use a section-job heading such as "How I got here" for the
origin story.
_Avoid_: Duplicating a personal greeting as both page title and section title.

**BASIC Laptop Story**:
Jason's first program: a Redwall choose-your-own-adventure game made at age
twelve on an old black-and-white-screen laptop while learning BASIC from an old
high school textbook.
_Avoid_: Calling it the first program he remembers making, dropping the BASIC
learning context, turning it into a polished origin myth.

**Adventure-Making Tools**:
The bridge from Jason's childhood imagination to Mythic: Legos, books,
imagination, video games, and later RPGs such as Shadowrun and Dungeons &
Dragons as tools for creating characters, exploring worlds, and telling
fantastic stories with yourself or friends.
_Avoid_: Treating the list as a fandom inventory, implying Mythic is only about
escapism, reducing the bridge to "I like fantasy," using RPG examples as
credentials.

**Germany Reset**:
The life transition after getting married, moving to Germany, and figuring out
what came after teaching. It explains why Jason was exploring practical software
business paths, including Shopify development, while still wanting to build
apps.
_Avoid_: Career failure framing, relocation detour, cutting the practical
business context, long explanation of teaching job constraints,
application-style career narrative.

**Brother's Mythic Introduction**:
The way Mythic entered Jason's life: his brother, who was more plugged into the
TTRPG community and knew Jason wanted to build apps, pointed him toward Tana
Pigeon's search for someone to build an app for Mythic.
_Avoid_: Implying Jason had long followed Mythic, making the brother a minor
aside, turning the story into networking luck, job-post wording.

**Cautious Mythic Discovery**:
Jason's initial posture toward the Mythic opportunity: interested, curious, and
reserved rather than immediately triumphant or certain.
_Avoid_: Destiny language, "Mythic became possible", pretending instant
confidence, overplaying skepticism.

**Licensing Opportunity**:
The moment Mythic became a real product path after Jason looked into the
opportunity: a chance to build digital tools for other people to tell stories,
connecting Jason's love of adventure-making tools with product experiences
people enjoy using.
_Avoid_: Pure business opportunity, career pivot framing, implying opportunism.

**Disappearing Tool**:
A product experience that helps players create and tell stories, then gets out
of the way while they play.
_Avoid_: Tooling as the point, feature worship, app-first storytelling,
generic "useful" language when the point is reducing friction in play.

**Extended Game-Making Stories**:
Additional early creative-code examples, such as making levels with the Descent
level editor, that belong in a longer bio or blog post rather than the main
About-page opening.
_Avoid_: Childhood media inventory, delaying the move from origin story to
Mythic, overloading the About page with every example.

**Redwall Story**:
Jason's specific early example of using code and imagination to explore a
beloved fictional world in a freer, more personal way.
_Avoid_: Hiding the name when specificity helps, fan-service aside,
overexplaining childhood, using the story as proof of product quality, phrasing
it as a grand revelation.

**Professional Story**:
Jason's broader teacher-to-builder, product-engineering, and agentic-workflow
story.
_Avoid_: Treating this as the primary homepage or About-page goal.

**Teacher-To-Builder Path**:
An optional secondary bio thread about teaching computer science before building
official Mythic apps for a real player community.
_Avoid_: About-page hook, career pivot as novelty, grandness, education
background as proof.

**Teaching Students To Create With Code**:
The years Jason spent helping students see that code could turn an imagined
idea into a real digital thing they could share with other people.
_Avoid_: Teaching product creation, unrelated education background, career gap.

**Product Builder**:
A person who takes responsibility for a real product across listening,
judgment, implementation, support, and improvement.
_Avoid_: Aspiring developer, hobbyist, just an indie dev, trendy self-brand.

**Product Engineer**:
The external role language for work that combines software engineering,
product judgment, player listening, business direction, support, and shipping.
_Avoid_: Loose title inflation, staff-equivalent framing, role label without
proof.

**Founder And Product Engineer**:
The professional title for Jason's Mythic work at Jason Holt Digital when the
site needs a concise role label.
_Avoid_: Lead product developer, CTO, founder-only framing.

**Only Product Engineer**:
The accurate scope claim that Jason is the only product engineer on the
licensed Mythic apps.
_Avoid_: Lead product engineer, team-lead implication, inflated seniority.

**Public Partner Name**:
The public partner name for the Mythic app license relationship is Word Mill
Creative.
_Avoid_: Normalizing public copy to Word Mill Games unless quoting source
material, legal attribution, or a verified DBA-specific context.

**Licensed Apps**:
The precise label for the Mythic GME companion apps because they are built under
licensed Mythic IP.
_Avoid_: Side project, hobby app, generic paid app, official when licensed is
the more precise point.

**License Once Then Move On**:
The About-page rule that official/licensed/partner wording should establish
trust once in the page header or profile, then give the rest of the page to the
story, community, product, and approach.
_Avoid_: Repeating license proof in a separate section, brochure-like official
copy, making the license the emotional center of About.

**Real Product Pressure**:
The combined responsibility of shipping licensed software, earning community
trust, supporting real players, and making the app work as a business.
_Avoid_: Pressure as stress only, proving myself, hustle, monetizing users.

**Community-First Work**:
The practice of listening to players, interpreting needs, supporting issues,
and improving the apps through repeated visible action.
_Avoid_: Taking orders, community management, feature-vote popularity contest.

**Community Trust**:
The confidence players develop when they see Jason listen, support the app,
complete visible work, and protect the product's direction.
_Avoid_: Buy-in as persuasion, relationship management, promotion, hype.

**Community Welcome**:
The Mythic community's early warmth toward Jason and the apps, which encouraged
him and continues to encourage him as he keeps building.
_Avoid_: Hype, generic gratitude, making community welcome sound like market
validation.

**Trust Through Repeated Action**:
The way credibility is earned by responding, asking questions, fixing bugs,
completing improvements, and showing up consistently.
_Avoid_: Trust-building as messaging, community marketing.

**Support Loop**:
The direct cycle of hearing a player's issue, asking questions, making sure
they feel understood, tracking or fixing the problem, and following up.
_Avoid_: Customer service script, ticket handling, user management.

**Feedback Loop**:
The visible path from player feedback through Discord, feature requests, public
wraps, release planning, support conversations, and completed product work.
_Avoid_: Feedback to shipping, shipping as a badge, vague feedback system.

**Completed Product Work**:
The plain phrase for player feedback becoming real product progress.
_Avoid_: Shipped work as a generic badge, launched as hype, progress without
evidence.

**Feature Request Trail**:
The visible history of player requests moving through discussion,
prioritization, implementation, and follow-up.
_Avoid_: Backlog, wishlist, suggestion box.

**Build In Public**:
Making product work visible to the Mythic Community through public starts,
completions, fixes, delays, and releases.
_Avoid_: Marketing, content strategy, performing productivity.

**Daily Wrap**:
A public Linear-backed summary of what Jason started, completed, fixed, or
carried forward in Mythic app work.
_Avoid_: Generic status update, changelog, social post.

**Current Community Availability**:
Jason is active on Discord, reachable by email, and responsive on the public
feature board at feedback.jasonholtdigital.com even though the old daily-wrap
cadence is no longer daily while he is finishing his master's.
_Avoid_: Claiming daily progress updates, implying the Discord daily-wrap
channel is currently active every day, saying Jason does not have time to work
on the apps every day, hiding that the master's has changed the pace.

**Product Judgment**:
The discipline of turning player feedback into the right product change rather
than the most literal request.
_Avoid_: Taste alone, automatic yes, dismissive no, building whatever users ask
for.

**Product Direction From Feedback**:
The broader design process of using player feedback to understand where the app
should go, what the experience needs to feel like, and where work should focus.
_Avoid_: Reducing feedback to bug fixes, feature triage, what to say no to, or
the next task list.

**Responsive Product Judgment**:
Knowing when to say yes, when to say no, how to say yes or no, and when to say
"I don't know; let me look into it."
_Avoid_: Community leadership as title, pretending certainty.

**Listening**:
Learning from players closely enough to understand the deeper need behind a
request.
_Avoid_: Taking orders, implementing every request, the customer is always
right.

**Feedback As Focus**:
The way rough, wild, or incomplete player ideas help Jason see what matters and
keep product work pointed at the right problems.
_Avoid_: Treating requests as instructions, dismissing ambitious ideas,
corporate ideation language.

**Product Experience**:
The quality of the app as players encounter it in real use, including trust,
taste, stability, accessibility, and fit to their flow of play.
_Avoid_: Usefulness, feature set, UI polish alone.

**Product Section Job**:
The About-page product section should explain how real sessions shape the work,
not repeat the license, community welcome, or full product inventory already
established elsewhere on the page.
_Avoid_: Repeating the Profile ending, feature list intro, app-store pitch.

**High Quality**:
The standard that the product should be stable, enjoyable, accessible,
trustworthy, and shaped to how players actually use it.
_Avoid_: Perfect, polished enough, quality as vague praise.

**Best-Way Aim**:
The product ambition to make the apps one of the best ways to play RPGs with
Mythic.
_Avoid_: Declaring the apps are the best way to play, dismissing analog play or
other tools, ranking the community's play styles.

**Agentic Engineering Practice**:
Jason's accountable use of AI-assisted workflows for specs, implementation,
review, tests, validation, and iteration.
_Avoid_: AI wrote my code, autonomous agents, prompt engineering as the point.

**Master's Rigor Layer**:
The way Jason's M.Sc. strengthens the mathematical, technical, and empirical
foundation behind language systems, retrieval, evaluation, and AI-assisted
engineering.
_Avoid_: Student positioning, credential decoration, implying senior ML
engineering experience.

**Master's Disclosure Note**:
A short About-page note that Jason is pursuing an M.Sc. in Computational
Linguistics while continuing to improve, plan, support, and listen to player
feedback on the Mythic apps. Its purpose is to give current context without
making the page defensive.
_Avoid_: Turning the About page into a resume, making the degree a main section,
using the degree as product proof, vague "real shape" phrasing, signposting the
sentence as "one practical note."

### Product and play

**Flow Of Play**:
The player's active creative rhythm while asking questions, interpreting
results, recording events, and continuing the story.
_Avoid_: App workflow, task flow, usage pattern.

**Playspace**:
The Mythic-first play environment the player inhabits across app surfaces.
_Avoid_: Workspace, generic app experience.

**Surface**:
The device and usage context the playspace adapts to, such as phone, tablet,
desktop, or web.
_Avoid_: Screen, viewport, form factor.

**Panel**:
A focused region of the playspace that supports a specific gameplay flow.
_Avoid_: Container, widget, screen.

**Journal**:
The app's persistent play context for one ongoing adventure or campaign by
default.
_Avoid_: Campaign when referring to the current in-product term, save slot.

**Adventure Log**:
The chronological record of play inside a Journal.
_Avoid_: Journal when only the chronology is meant, scene log.

**Scene Summary**:
A Mythic record that summarizes or frames a Scene without being the
chronological source of truth.
_Avoid_: Scene when precision matters, Adventure Log.

**Fate Question**:
A yes-or-no question posed to Mythic and answered through the current
fate-answering system.
_Avoid_: Prompt, generic question.

**Meaning Tables**:
Mythic tables that generate interpretive prompts to help the player decide what
results mean in context.
_Avoid_: Fixed result tables, outcome tables.

**Custom Tables**:
Player-created or imported rollable tables that make personal or third-party
oracle content feel native to the apps.
_Avoid_: Add-on data, import gimmick, generic table files.

**Integrated Reference Layer**:
The product interpretation that PDFs, character sheets, maps, rules references,
and play assets should live close to the playspace rather than as disconnected
attachments.
_Avoid_: PDF attachment as the whole feature, file viewer bolted onto the app,
static reference dump.

**Portable Player Data**:
Player-owned journals, tables, exports, or vault data that can be backed up,
shared, inspected, or moved outside the app.
_Avoid_: Opaque app database, app state, cloud-only save.
